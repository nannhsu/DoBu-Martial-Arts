Karate

Karate is a Japanese martial art that involves unarmed combat techniques such as striking, kicking, and blocking.
It originated in Okinawa, an island off Japan, and is known for its emphasis on self-defense, balance, and self-discipline.
The word "karate" is a combination of the Chinese characters kara, meaning "empty", and te, meaning "hand".

Muay Thai

Muay Thai, also known as Thai boxing, is a full-contact combat sport and martial art that originated in Thailand.
It's characterized by the use of eight points of contact, or limbs, to strike an opponent, including punches, elbows, knees, and shins.

Jiu-jitsu

Jiu-jitsu is a martial art and combat sport that focuses on grappling and ground fighting.
The term "jiu-jitsu" comes from Japanese and means "gentle art".
Jiu-jitsu uses leverage, angles, pressure, and timing to control and submit an opponent. The goal is to gain the upper hand without striking your opponent.
